Trash Guide

Trash can be of many kind. Below is an attempt to categorise and advise for every one of them.


  • Paper/Carton/Cardboard
  • Plastic
  • Metal
  • E-Waste
  • Textile Waste
  • Glass
  • Organic Waste




Organic Waste

Examples that can be composted are:

  • Banana peels
  • Hair
  • Flowers
  • Leaves
  • Excrements from non-carnivorous animals

Examples that cannot be composted are:

  • Diseased Plants
  • Excrements from carnivorous animals
  • Dairy Products
  • Meat Products
  • Bread Products - e.g. Baked cakes
  • Cooking oil

For more information on composting: Mission Verte




Paper/Cardboard/Cartons

Recyclable:

  • Dry and clean paper/cardboards/Cartons
  • Books & Magazines
  • Paper bags/Brown paper bags
  • Egg trays

Not recyclable

  • Tissue paper & paper towels
  • Oily Papers and Cardboards e.g. pizza boxes
  • wet or soiled paper/cardboard/paper bags
  • cardboard lined with plastic (i.e. bubblewrap boxes)
  • waxed/waterproof cardboard

Plastic

Recyclable:

  • Any plastic product with the PET/PETE sign: PET bottle recycling.

    Bottles made of polyethylene terephthalate (PET, sometimes PETE) can be "recycled" to reuse the material out of which they are made and to reduce the amount of waste going into landfills.

  • Plastic water/soft drink/milk bottles
  • Cleaned and rinsed shampoo/detergent bottles

Not recyclable

  • Laminated/Plastified paper

Glass

Glass is one of the few materials that can be recycled infinitely without losing strength, purity or quality. It is a widely recycled material, but beware: broken glass can be painful for both you and the recycling process. Broken glass is recyclable and may be turned into new products like fiberglass, tile and flooring. However, broken glass cannot be placed inside the recycle bin because it could injure sanitation workers. - Earth911
